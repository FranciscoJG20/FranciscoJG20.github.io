About Them


Francisco is a Brooklyn-based writer and software engineer.

His varying work experience — from grassroots organizing for a political organization to leading corporate training workshops and introducing employees to emerging technologies — informed Francisco's eagerness to amplify human stories currently ignored by mainstream media. Francisco has recently written and led content development for 2050Work, a job board specifically for job seekers of color in tech and creative. He previously wrote for San Francisco paper, El Tecolote, where he started his own column, "Latinxs Redefining Tech" and highlighted the positive impact that Latinos (gender-inclusive) are having in the technology industry. Additionally, Francisco has written about technology and his interview with Oracle DevOps Engineer was published in Faun, a growing community of devops, full-stack engineers and IT professionals.

Francisco holds a B.S. in Marketing from Georgetown University's McDonough School of Business and is a former employee and alumnus of General Assembly.
